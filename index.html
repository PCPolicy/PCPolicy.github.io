Zero-Shot Cross-Embodiment Grasping with PCA-based Policy

Paper Video Code Data

Abstract

Generalizing grasping policies to manipulators with unseen morphologies remains a key challenge in robotic manipulation. While prior work demonstrates that shared postural synergy representations enhance learning efficiency, their generalization to novel manipulators remains constrained. In this paper, we propose PCPolicy, a cross-embodiment grasping policy that improves zero-shot generalization to novel manipulators. Building on anchor-based geometric descriptions, PCPolicy introduces a morphology-aware latent synergy space that disentangles grasp pose from manipulator embodiment. This disentanglement allows easy integration of novel manipulators into the same policy during inference. We evaluate PCPolicy on grasping tasks involving both seen and unseen manipulators and objects, ranging from parallel grippers to multi-fingered dexterous hands. PCPolicy achieves substantially higher zero-shot grasp success on novel manipulators while preserving performance on seen ones. These results highlight the importance of explicitly decoupling grasp pose from manipulator morphology for scalable and generalizable grasp learning.
